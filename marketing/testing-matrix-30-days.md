# 30-Day Content Testing Matrix

## Goal

Build the first repeatable content-learning loop for littlecolorbook.com.

The goal of the first 30 days is not maximum scale. The goal is to find:

- the best hook families
- the best product-proof formats
- the best-performing offers
- the best personas and voice families
- the best occasion frames
- the strongest path from free sample to print and sibling bundle purchase

## Success Targets For The First 30 Days

- landing page opt-in rate: at least 20%
- sample-to-purchase rate: at least 10%
- print attach rate: at least 35%
- sibling bundle attach rate on print: at least 25%
- creator and affiliate CAC: under $20
- cold paid free-sample CPL: under $6
- at least 5 winning hook families
- at least 3 winning product-proof formats
- at least 2 occasion frames worth expanding

## Daily Output Targets

Each weekday:

- 12 to 18 raw creative variants
- 4 to 6 organic posts queued or published
- 2 to 4 paid nominations
- 3 to 5 descendants of known winners

Weekly:

- 25 or more published short-form posts
- 10 to 15 paid-ready creatives
- 1 occasion-specific campaign pack
- 1 creator or affiliate brief refresh
- 1 synthesis report

## Testing Framework

### Variable groups

Only vary one major variable at a time inside a descendant batch.

Major variable groups:

- hook family
- opening visual
- persona
- avatar
- voice family
- CTA
- occasion framing
- format
- offer focus

### Baseline offer priority

Use this weighting for the first month:

- 40% Free Sample Page
- 25% Solo Keepsake
- 15% Sibling Set
- 10% 30-Design PDF
- 10% other offers combined

### Format priority

Use this weighting for the first month:

- 35% before and after proof
- 20% proof montage
- 15% creator talking head with product cutaways
- 10% sibling bundle angle
- 10% print flip-through
- 10% FAQ and objection handling

## Week 1: Product Truth And Hook Discovery

### Objective

Find the strongest raw hooks and visual openings.

### Themes

- photo to coloring page transformation
- kids seeing their own memories in the book
- screen-free activity angle
- generic coloring book comparison angle

### Primary offers

- Free Sample Page
- Solo Keepsake

### Test families

1. Hook family A: surprise transformation
   - "I turned our family photo into a coloring page"
   - "This starts as a normal photo"
2. Hook family B: emotional reveal
   - "My daughter saw herself in her coloring book"
   - "This is why personalized pages beat generic books"
3. Hook family C: screen-free utility
   - "The easiest low-screen activity we have found"
   - "What I use when I need 20 quiet minutes"
4. Hook family D: proof-first comparison
   - "Generic coloring books versus your own memories"

### Daily plan

- Day 1: test 4 hook families against the same before-and-after format
- Day 2: keep top 2 hook families, vary opening visuals
- Day 3: test 3 voice families against the top 2 hooks
- Day 4: test Warm Millennial Mom versus Organized Practical Mom personas
- Day 5: create 5 descendants from the top 3 winners and nominate 2 for paid

## Week 2: Offer And Conversion Discovery

### Objective

Find which offers convert best from cold traffic and from free-sample retargeting.

### Themes

- free sample CTA
- print versus PDF
- Solo Keepsake versus Sibling Set
- giftability

### Primary offers

- Free Sample Page
- Solo Keepsake
- Sibling Set
- 30-Design PDF

### Test families

1. Free sample direct response
2. Solo Keepsake direct response
3. Sibling Set no-fighting angle
4. print quality and spiral format proof
5. PDF speed and convenience angle

### Daily plan

- Day 6: free sample versus direct-to-product CTA
- Day 7: Solo Keepsake versus Sibling Set angle using same core visuals
- Day 8: print-first versus PDF-first ad framing
- Day 9: FAQ creatives around upload effort, timing, and print quality
- Day 10: descendants of top offer combinations, promote best 2 to paid

## Week 3: Occasion Discovery

### Objective

Find which occasion frames materially lift response without breaking the core brand.

### Themes

- birthdays
- vacations
- grandparent gifts
- rainy day activity
- sibling conflict

### Primary offers

- Free Sample Page
- Solo Keepsake
- Sibling Set

### Daily plan

- Day 11: birthday angle set
- Day 12: vacation memory angle set
- Day 13: grandparent gift angle set
- Day 14: rainy day and travel activity angle set
- Day 15: compare best occasion winner against evergreen control

### Occasion scoring rule

An occasion graduates if it beats evergreen on at least 2 of:

- hook rate
- CTR
- opt-in rate
- CAC
- print attach

## Week 4: Scale Preparation And RL Tightening

### Objective

Turn the best concepts into repeatable systems for organic, paid, and creator use.

### Themes

- creator adaptation of winners
- paid edits of winners
- localization candidates

### Daily plan

- Day 16: make 5 descendants from each top-performing winner cluster
- Day 17: create creator-style versions in Arcads from top 3 winning scripts
- Day 18: create Meta-friendly paid edits of organic winners
- Day 19: test Spanish dubbing on the top English winner
- Day 20: compare creator wrapper versus proof montage versus direct-response edit

## Days 21 To 30: Exploit And Systemize

### Objective

Use the last 10 days to shift from exploration into scaled exploitation.

### Rules

- 70% exploit current winners
- 20% adjacent tests
- 10% new bets

### Day grouping

- Days 21 to 23: exploit top evergreen winners
- Days 24 to 26: exploit top occasion winners
- Days 27 to 28: exploit top bundle and print winners
- Day 29: compile monthly synthesis and winner library
- Day 30: produce next-month plan with kill, scale, and localize recommendations

## Kill Rules For The 30-Day Test

Kill a line of testing early if:

- 3 variants in the same family miss hook-rate baseline badly
- comments indicate distrust or confusion about whether the product is real
- bundle framing is confusing instead of helpful
- the creative cannot produce a clear product-proof opening
- metrics are weak and there is no plausible adjacent fix

## Scale Rules For The 30-Day Test

Scale a line of testing if:

- at least 2 variants in the family win on the same core angle
- the winning family improves either contribution economics or strong leading indicators
- the concept can be repurposed across organic, paid, and creator formats
- the concept preserves product truth and can be repeated cheaply
