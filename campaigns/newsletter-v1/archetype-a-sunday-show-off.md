# Archetype A — Sunday Show-Off

Generated 2026-04-17 by `/newsletter`

---

## The Feel

This is the main event. One family. One photo. One finished page. A short story with enough warmth that a reader thinks, "that's us in three months."

Sunday 6pm. The kitchen's quiet, the kids are winding down, the reader is scrolling on the couch. This email should feel like a weekly episode they now watch.

Visual density: low. Copy length: medium (~180 words total). Tone: warm, specific, settled.

## The Template

### Subject Line Slot

Pick one of the three variants below. Aim for under 42 characters for mobile previews. No emoji spam. No all caps.

**A/B test**: variant ① vs variant ② every send. Rotate ③ in every 3rd week.

**Three variants per send.**

```
① This week's page: {childName} and the {subject}
② What {childName}'s mom printed this weekend
③ The {subject} photo that became a page
```

Examples with live values:

- "This week's page: Mila and the muddy boots"
- "What Ava's mom printed this weekend"
- "The beach-day photo that became a page"

More variants in `copy-patterns.md`.

### Preheader

One line. Sets up the emotional hook before the reader opens.

```
A family favorite, turned into something they can color tonight.
```

Or:

```
One photo from {location}. One page for the coloring book.
```

### Hero (above the fold)

A side-by-side split.

```
┌────────────────────┬────────────────────┐
│                    │                    │
│   original photo   │   finished page    │
│   (slightly soft   │   (crisp line art, │
│    parent-real)    │    on white)       │
│                    │                    │
└────────────────────┴────────────────────┘

      The {childName} {subject} page
```

On mobile, stack vertically (photo on top, page below). Each image sized 1:1 at 375px minimum. No filters, no watermarks, no frames. Let the contrast do the work.

Caption below, one line, lowercase, serif or light weight:

```
from the camera roll → in the coloring book
```

### This Week's Featured Family

Three sentences max. Second person singular — you're telling the reader about one specific family, like you're passing along a note.

```
This is {childName}, {ageOrContext}. Her mom
uploaded this photo from {momentContext}. The
page arrived in her {packageSize}-page book
last {dayOfWeek}.
```

Example:

```
This is Mila, almost 4. Her mom uploaded this
photo from their rainy-Sunday muddy boots walk.
The page arrived in her 50-page book last
Tuesday.
```

Consent required. The name is the child's first name only. No age published beyond approximate ("almost 4," "toddler years," "turning 7"). No city, no school, no last name. Rules in `content-pipeline.md`.

### What Makes This Work

One sentence. A micro-caption explaining why the page turned out great. This is the "smart friend over your shoulder" moment.

Examples:

```
The high-contrast light made the boots and
her profile translate almost one-to-one.
```

```
A clear face and a single prop — the rubber
duck — is often all a great page needs.
```

```
Overcast days actually turn into better
pages than bright sun.
```

Keep this human. Never say "our AI" or "the system." The voice here is the capable mom friend who has seen a thousand of these.

```
└──────────────────────────────┘
```

Button copy bank (rotate):

```
- Start with a sample →
- Upload a photo →
- Make one from your camera roll →
- See yours as a page →
```

No "Buy now." No "Shop." No "Save 20%." This is a show, not a shop — the soft CTA is the sponsor slot, not the whole email.

### P.S. (this week's small wins)

Bottom of the email. One short line with a specific, real number. This is where you remind the reader there's a real community around this product.

```
P.S. {number} new pages shipped last week.
     Next Sunday: another family.
```

Examples:

```
P.S. 234 new pages shipped last week.
     Next Sunday: another family.
```

```
P.S. 18 grandma copies went out Friday alone.
     See you Thursday for the gallery.
```

```

     4,200 photos on it. One became a page.
     Next Sunday: another family.
```

## Why This Archetype Works

- **One thing.** The reader does not have to choose. There is no menu. There is a family, and it is this one.
- **Proof without selling.** By featuring a real family, the email is social proof and product demo at once — but it never says "see how it works."
- **Emotional anchor.** The 2-3 sentence story makes the reader imagine their own photo in that slot. That's the whole selling motion, done softly.
- **Ritual.** Sunday 6pm, every week, same shape, different family. It becomes a show.

## Off-Brand Failure Modes (Do Not Write)

```
✗ "This week in Little Color Book..." — sounds like a corporate recap
✗ "Meet the Mila family!" — gushy, not specific
✗ "Our amazing AI turned..." — operator language
✗ "Upgrade to 100 pages today!" — wrong register for this email
✗ Multiple photos of the same family — dilutes the single-hero focus
✗ A founder quote at the bottom — customer doesn't want to hear from Alec here
```
