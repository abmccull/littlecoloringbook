## Email Copy (Plain Text)

{{first_name | default:"Hi"}},

A few ideas parents have written in with. Stealing them so you have them too:

**1. The road trip stack.**
Tear out 6-8 pages, clip them with a binder clip, toss a crayon box in the console. Kid has a screen-free activity that doesn't involve juggling a whole book in the car.

**2. The chore chart swap.**
One family uses colored pages as reward tokens. Make your bed, pick a page. Works especially well if the pages are of the kid themselves — they get weirdly proud of coloring in their own face.

**3. The bedtime wind-down.**
Ten minutes of coloring beats ten minutes of iPad before sleep. Keep the book on the nightstand.

**4. The sick day kit.**
Pair the book with the crayons, a juice box, and a blanket. Nobody's happy when they're sick, but this helps. Especially for kids who want quiet but not quite sleep.

**5. The "when mom needs 20 minutes" kit.**
No shame in it. You put your headphones in, they color a page of the dog. Everyone wins.

No ask on this one. Just wanted you to have the list.

— Little Color Book

P.S. If you've invented your own use, hit reply. We add the best ones to the list.
