# Founder-story angle — Meta ad copy

Testing the dad-of-three founder story as an acquisition angle. Core hypothesis: parents scrolling Meta respond to another parent's specific frustration more than to feature copy.

Voice: casual-confident, first-person, specific scenes over claims. No em-dashes, no operator language, no hedging.

Landing page: `/sample` (free sample funnel). Primary CTA text: "See your photo become a coloring page in about 30 seconds."

Optimization event: **Purchase** (sales objective, not leads — see tasks/lessons.md entry on optimizing for revenue, not the midway event).

---

## Primary text — 5 variants

### A. Long-form story (~620 chars)

> I'm a dad of three. For the thousandth time my kids asked me to print coloring pages off Google.
>
> Fifteen minutes of a six-year-old scrolling. Watermarks. Low-res. Pages that don't fit on the paper.
>
> So I started feeding our family photos to AI instead. The kids. The dog. My son holding a stick like it's Excalibur.
>
> Turned them into real coloring pages. Bold lines. Clean backgrounds. No logos.
>
> My kids lost it. They stopped asking for Pokemon and started staging their own scenes. Suddenly they were the heroes.
>
> That became Little Color Book. Try one page free from a photo on your phone. Under 30 seconds.

### B. Hook-first with scroll-stop opener (~550 chars)

> For the thousandth time my kids asked me to print a Pokemon coloring page off Google. So I did this instead.
>
> I'm a dad of three. Tired of the 15-minute scrolling contest for which dragon has the coolest mouth. Tired of watermarks. Tired of pages that don't fit on the paper.
>
> I started feeding our family photos to AI. Turned them into real coloring pages with my kids as the heroes.
>
> My kids stopped asking for Pokemon.
>
> Try one free from a photo on your phone. 30 seconds.

### C. Short + punchy (~320 chars)

> Fifteen minutes of watching my six-year-old scroll through dragon coloring pages on Google.
>
> That was the last straw.
>
> Now I feed our family photos to AI and turn them into coloring pages with my kids as the heroes. They stopped asking for Pokemon.
>
> See your own photo become a coloring page. Free, 30 seconds.

### D. The specific moment (~420 chars)

> My six-year-old spent fifteen minutes picking a dragon coloring page on Google. Then the printer spit out a watermark across its face.
>
> I built Little Color Book so my kids could be the hero of the page. Not some stock dragon on page 47 of a search result.
>
> Upload one favorite photo. Get a free coloring page in 30 seconds. If your kid lights up, the rest of your camera roll becomes the book.

### E. Parent-to-parent direct (~380 chars)

> Fellow parents: if your kid has ever decision-paralyzed for 15 minutes picking a coloring page off Google, this is for you.
>
> I turned our family photos into coloring pages where my kids were the heroes. They stopped asking for Pokemon. They colored for hours. No screens. No negotiation.
>
> Free sample in 30 seconds from a photo on your phone.

## Headlines — 6 variants (40-char target)

1. ★ My kids stopped asking for Pokemon
2. I quit printing Google coloring pages
3. A dad of three built this
4. 15 minutes of dragon-scrolling ended
5. Your kid as the coloring page hero
6. Coloring pages from your camera roll

Recommend **#1** as lead headline — it's a story-tease, creates curiosity (why?), and names the specific enemy (Pokemon/Google search fatigue).

## Test plan

### Priority HIGH — test these first

**Test 1: Primary text angle — Founder story vs Benefit-forward control**
- Why: Hypothesis is that founder vulnerability + specific scene (Pokemon/Excalibur/page 47) outperforms generic "turn photos into coloring pages" benefit copy on Meta, where scroll-stop is everything.
- Variants to run: A (long), C (short), plus one non-founder control ad using the current homepage hero copy.
- Metric: CTR → `/sample`, CPA to Purchase.
- Budget: $30/day per variant, 3 days, 2 creatives per variant (rotating).

**Test 2: Hook opener — "thousandth time" vs "fifteen minutes" vs "fellow parents"**
- Why: The first 125 chars above the "See more" fold determines scroll-stop.
- Variants: A opens with "thousandth time", C opens with "fifteen minutes", E opens with "Fellow parents". Same body otherwise.
- Metric: 3-second video view rate (if video) or scroll-stop rate, CTR.

### Priority MEDIUM

**Test 3: Primary text length — Long (A) vs Short (C)**
- Why: Long-form founder stories often over-perform short ones when the story is specific, but only after the hook is proven.
- Metric: CTR, CPA. Run this AFTER Test 1 picks a winner hook.

**Test 4: Headline — "My kids stopped asking for Pokemon" vs "Your kid as the coloring page hero"**
- Why: Curiosity tease vs benefit statement. Meta learns fast on 30-char headline swaps.
- Metric: CTR on same primary text body.

### Priority LOW (after the above are optimized)

**Test 5: Description CTA — "Free sample in 30 seconds" vs "Free sample, no card"**
- Why: Specificity of time vs risk-reversal framing.
- Metric: CTA click-through.

## Creative pairings

Founder-story ads pair best with one of:
- Real family photo → coloring page before/after (the Excalibur-stick energy)
- A still of Alec's kids coloring a self-portrait page (if shootable)
- 8-10 second video of the generation itself (photo uploaded, coloring page appears) narrated or captioned with hook line

Avoid: stock family photos, generic illustration, any creative that doesn't feel like a dad's phone camera roll.

## Notes for future iteration

- If founder-story angle wins Test 1, commission a 15-second founder video using Variant A as the script. Dad-to-camera UGC will outperform static ads in this category.
- The "cat pulled into the plot against her will" detail is the funniest line; consider surfacing it in a longer variant or video caption.
- "Page 47 of a search result" is the most-specific line — reserve for one headline variant.
- Once we have 20+ real customer samples, replace one founder-story variant with a user-story variant ("My daughter saw her own face on the page and...").
