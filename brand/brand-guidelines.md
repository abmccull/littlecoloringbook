# Little Color Book Brand Guidelines

Version: `v1`
Date: `2026-04-10`

This document defines the current Little Color Book brand based on the product, funnel, copy system, and visual language already implemented in the site.

It is meant to keep future landing pages, emails, ads, product UI, and creative work consistent.

## 1. Brand Core

### Brand Name

`Little Color Book`

### Brand Promise

Turn the favorite photos already on a family's phone into a screen-free activity a child will want to use now and a keepsake the family will want to keep later.

### What We Actually Sell

We do not sell `AI image generation`.

We sell:

- an easy screen-free win for moms with young kids
- a personalized activity that feels special without becoming a project
- a giftable keepsake made from real family photos

### Emotional Job

The brand should make a parent feel:

- this is easy enough to do tonight
- this is personal enough to feel thoughtful
- this is good enough to keep, gift, or reorder

## 2. Audience

### Primary Buyer

Moms with young kids.

Especially moms who:

- want low-prep screen-free activities
- want something personal, not generic
- are often browsing on mobile from Instagram or TikTok
- do not want another craft project or complicated customization flow

### Secondary Buyer

Grandparents, gift buyers, and relatives buying for birthdays, holidays, or family keepsakes.

### Audience Reality

The buyer is not looking for a technical product. She is looking for an easy yes.

That means the brand must always reduce friction before it adds detail.

## 3. Positioning

### Primary Positioning Angle

`Screen-Free Mom Win`

Turn the photos already on your phone into a screen-free activity your kid will actually use and a keepsake you'll actually keep.

### Supporting Angles

- `Personalized Without Becoming A Project`
- `Giftable Keepsake From The Camera Roll`

### Competitive Difference

The category often sounds generic: `custom`, `personalized`, `made from your photos`.

Little Color Book should feel more:

- emotionally useful
- lower-friction
- more modern
- more family-real

## 4. Brand Personality

### Core Traits

- `Warm without being gushy`
- `Confident without sounding salesy`
- `Playful premium`
- `Specific over generic`
- `Low-friction`

### Brand Temperature

Little Color Book should feel:

- bright, not loud
- premium, not luxury-stiff
- kid-adjacent, not preschool-cheap
- emotionally real, not sentimental mush

## 5. Voice And Tone

### Voice Summary

Little Color Book sounds like the capable mom friend who already found the easiest thoughtful thing to do with the photos on your phone.

The voice is warm, bright, direct, and specific. It should never sound like a startup landing page generator, an internal product document, or a kids brand talking down to adults.

### Tone Spectrum

- Formal ↔ Casual: `Casual`
- Serious ↔ Playful: `Playful, but restrained`
- Reserved ↔ Bold: `Confident`
- Simple ↔ Sophisticated: `Simple on the surface, premium underneath`
- Warm ↔ Direct: `Warm-direct`

### Writing Rules

- Lead with the outcome first.
- Explain convenience second.
- Use proof third.
- Prefer short-to-medium sentences.
- Use contractions freely.
- Keep paragraphs compact and mobile-friendly.
- Use concrete family examples instead of abstract claims.

### Vocabulary To Use Often

- favorite photos
- camera roll
- screen-free
- quiet time
- keepsake
- giftable
- print tonight
- spiral book
- easy yes
- personal
- made from your photos
- rainy afternoon
- grandma copy
- sibling copy

### Vocabulary To Avoid

- funnel
- workflow
- selected offer
- queued
- quality bar
- pipeline
- SKU
- conversion path
- proof step
- internal logic

## 6. Messaging Hierarchy

When writing customer-facing copy, use this order:

1. `Outcome`
   Example: turn favorite photos into a coloring book they will actually use
2. `Ease`
   Example: no design project, no perfect-photo hunt
3. `Proof`
   Example: start with one free sample page first
4. `Format`
   Example: print at home tonight or order the spiral-bound keepsake

### Core Message Pillars

- `Screen-free activity`
- `Personal from your real photos`
- `Easy enough for tonight`
- `Good enough to keep or gift`

## 7. Offer And Funnel Framing

### Funnel Role

The homepage sells the `free sample` first.

The sample exists to make the full-book purchase feel low-risk and obvious.

### Offer Ladder

- `10 pages` = mini starter
- `30 pages` = good starter
- `50 pages` = better choice
- `100 pages` = best-value keepsake

### Format Labels

- `Print Tonight PDF`
- `Giftable Spiral Book + PDF`

### Merchandising Rule

`30` should stay visible.

`50` should feel like the stronger middle choice.

`100` should be framed as the fullest and best-value version when the camera roll is already packed.

## 8. Visual Identity

### Brand Direction

`Playful Premium`

The site should feel energetic, warm, and modern while still polished enough to support a gift purchase.

### Color Palette

- `Cream` `#FFF8F2`
- `Paper` `#FFFDFC`
- `Ink` `#241813`
- `Coral` `#D95B42`
- `Apricot` `#F8C8AA`
- `Sunshine` `#F6D660`
- `Mint` `#72C8A0`
- `Sky` `#9ADAF5`
- `Cocoa` `#7D4D3B`
- `Fog` `#F4E7DA`

### Color Use Rules

- `Cream` and `Paper` are the base surfaces.
- `Ink` is the primary text color and should stay rich and readable.
- `Coral` is the primary action color.
- `Sunshine`, `Mint`, and `Sky` are support accents for badges, merchandising, and sectional variety.
- Avoid flat, cold, corporate-looking white-gray compositions.

### Typography

- Display: `Bree Serif`
- Body/UI: `Nunito Sans`
- Accent/annotation: `Patrick Hand`

### Typography Rules

- Use `Bree Serif` for emotional headlines and offer titles.
- Use `Nunito Sans` for all UI, body text, forms, and support copy.
- Use `Patrick Hand` sparingly for notes, annotations, and playful supporting moments.
- Do not default to generic SaaS typography or overuse script-like treatments.

### Shapes And Surfaces

- Rounded corners should feel soft and inviting, not bubbly and childish.
- Use layered paper-like surfaces, warm gradients, and lifted cards.
- Avoid glassmorphism, overly frosted surfaces, or uniform same-size cards everywhere.

## 9. Imagery And Art Direction

### Image Priority

Real product proof should lead the visual story.

That means:

- real family photo
- transformed coloring page
- printed spiral book mockup

### What Imagery Should Feel Like

- bright
- warm
- clean
- personal
- family-real

### What To Avoid

- generic stock-mom lifestyle shots as the primary proof
- abstract doodles replacing real product proof
- over-styled luxury photography that loses the family-use feeling
- visuals that make the product feel like a novelty gimmick

## 10. UX And Funnel Principles

### UX Mood

The experience should feel guided, calm, and encouraging.

It should never feel like:

- software setup
- an internal dashboard
- a settings panel
- an order management tool shown too early

### Interaction Rules

- one primary job per page
- one primary CTA per key screen
- guest-first checkout
- mobile-first layout and copy
- progress should feel guided, not operational

### Mobile Rule

Design for the phone first.

Most users will likely discover the brand from mobile ads and complete the entire flow from their phone, including photo uploads.

## 11. CTA Style

CTA copy should be:

- direct
- warm
- specific
- low-friction

### Good CTA Examples

- `Get My Free Sample Page`
- `Build My Book`
- `Choose 100 Pages`
- `Go to Secure Checkout`
- `Show Delivery Choices`

### Bad CTA Examples

- `Submit`
- `Continue`
- `Select Offer`
- `Start Workflow`
- `Proceed to Funnel Step`

## 12. On-Brand Copy Examples

### On-Brand

- Turn the favorite photos already on your phone into a coloring book they will want to use tonight.
- No design project. No perfect-photo hunt. Just favorite moments turned into pages.
- An easy screen-free win for now, and a keepsake for later.
- Print it at home tonight or turn it into a spiral-bound gift.

### Off-Brand

- Leverage your family memories into a personalized activity asset.
- Our workflow converts source photos into printable outputs.
- Select the optimal package based on quantity and fulfillment preference.
- We use AI to generate custom pages from your uploaded source material.

## 13. Brand Guardrails

Always do this:

- make the brand feel easy before making it feel impressive
- sound like you understand real parent energy and time constraints
- write like a person, not a committee
- keep the copy and layouts mobile-scannable
- show real proof before long explanation

Never do this:

- sound like boring SaaS software
- sound juvenile or preschool-cheap
- lead with technical process
- over-explain the AI
- use internal business language in customer-facing copy

## 14. Current Gaps

Until those exist, this document should be the source of truth.
