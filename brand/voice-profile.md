## Little Color Book Voice Profile

Generated 2026-04-10 by `/brand-voice` via `/start-here`

### Voice Summary

Little Color Book sounds like the capable mom friend who already found the easiest thoughtful thing to do with the photos on your phone. The voice is warm, bright, and specific. It never sounds corporate, over-explained, or overly cute. It should feel premium, practical, and emotionally real.

This brand does not sell "AI image generation." It sells an easy family win: a personalized activity kids actually want to use and a keepsake adults actually want to keep.

### Personality Traits

- `Warm without being gushy`
  The tone feels caring and human, but never syrupy.
- `Confident without sounding salesy`
  It states the value clearly instead of over-hyping it.
- `Playful premium`
  It feels fun, kid-adjacent, and creative while still polished enough to justify a gift purchase.
- `Specific over generic`
  It uses real family contexts like rainy afternoons, birthday bags, grandma copies, camera rolls, and quiet time.
- `Low-friction`
  It constantly reduces the sense that this is "one more thing" for a parent to manage.

### Tone Spectrum

- Formal ↔ Casual: `Casual`
- Serious ↔ Playful: `Playful, but restrained`
- Reserved ↔ Bold: `Confident`
- Simple ↔ Sophisticated: `Simple on the surface, premium underneath`
- Warm ↔ Direct: `Warm-direct`

### Rhythm And Structure

- Prefer short-to-medium sentences.
- Lead with the outcome, then the convenience, then the proof.
- Use contractions freely.
- Keep paragraphs compact and mobile-friendly.
- Do not stack three abstract claims in a row.
- Use real family scenarios to make the product feel concrete.

### Signature Patterns

- `Outcome first`
  "Turn the photos already on your phone into..."
- `Ease + emotion`
  "Easy enough for tonight. Sweet enough to keep."
- `Now + later`
  "Something they can color now and you can save later."
- `Specific family context`
  "For rainy afternoons, birthday gifts, and grandma copies."

### On-Brand vs Off-Brand

#### On-Brand

- "Turn the photos already on your phone into a coloring book your kid will actually want to use."
- "An easy screen-free win for today, and a keepsake for later."
- "No design project. No perfect-photo hunt. Just favorite moments turned into pages."

#### Off-Brand

- "Leverage your family memories into a personalized activity asset."
- "Our workflow converts source photos into printable outputs."
- "Select the optimal package based on quantity and fulfillment preference."

### Platform Adaptation

| Platform | Voice Shift |
| --- | --- |
| Homepage | Most emotionally clear and benefit-first |
| Funnel pages | Shorter, more directional, less poetic |
| Builder | Calm, guiding, decisive |
| Email | Slightly more intimate and conversational |
| Paid social | Punchier hook, faster payoff, stronger camera-roll language |

### Do

- Sound like you understand real parent energy and time constraints.
- Make the product feel easy before you make it feel impressive.
- Use the words `screen-free`, `favorite photo`, `camera roll`, `print tonight`, and `giftable` naturally.
- Keep the copy visually scannable on mobile.

### Don't

- Sound like a startup landing page generator.
- Explain the product like an internal PRD.
- Over-index on AI, automation, or process language.
- Sound juvenile or preschool-cheap.
